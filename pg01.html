About the Fellowship

The Tele-Geriatric Research Fellowship is a one-year program dedicated to training the next generation of researchers and clinicians in the field of technology-enabled elder care. Our mission is to address the unique challenges faced by older adults in underserved areas.

We offer a comprehensive curriculum that integrates cutting-edge research methodologies with practical applications, allowing fellows to develop expertise in areas like remote patient monitoring, digital health interventions, and health equity in aging populations.

Fellowship Highlights:

  • Mentorship from leading experts in gerontology and telemedicine.
  • Opportunities for publication and grant writing.
  • Hands-on experience with emerging health technologies.
  • Focus on rural and underserved populations.

Curriculum & Research Areas

Remote Patient Monitoring

Focus on the development and evaluation of sensor-based technologies and telehealth platforms for chronic disease management in older adults.

AI & Machine Learning in Geriatrics

Explore the application of artificial intelligence to predict health outcomes, personalize care plans, and analyze large datasets in geriatric populations.

Health Equity & Access

Examine the social determinants of health and design telehealth interventions that reduce health disparities and improve access for rural and minority elders.

Clinical Trial Design

Hands-on training in designing, implementing, and analyzing clinical trials for tele-geriatric interventions, with a focus on ethical considerations.

Cognitive Health & Technology

Research on how technology can be used to assess, monitor, and support cognitive function in older adults with or at risk for dementia.

Policy & Advocacy

Understand the policy landscape of telehealth and learn to advocate for policies that support widespread adoption and reimbursement of digital health services.
